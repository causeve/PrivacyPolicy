Privacy Policy for VR Simulator Control Panel

Last updated: June 22, 2025

Thank you for using VR Simulator Control Panel (the “App”). Your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains:

  1. Information We Collect
  2. How We Use Your Information
  3. Sharing & Disclosure
  4. Security
  5. Your Choices
  6. Children’s Privacy
  7. Changes to this Policy
  8. Contact Us

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Bluetooth Data

  • We use CoreBluetooth to scan for and connect to your VR actuators.
  • No personal or location data is derived from Bluetooth.

1.2 Analytics (Optional)

  • We may collect anonymous usage metrics (e.g. feature usage, crash reports) via Apple’s built-in tools or a third-party SDK.
  • These data do not identify you personally.

1.3 Authentication

  • A simple username (“admin”) is stored in your device’s Keychain.
  • No cloud-based accounts or external servers are contacted.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • Bluetooth: to discover and control your VR hardware in real-time.
  • Analytics: to improve app stability and user experience.
  • Keychain: to persist your login securely on your device.

3. Sharing & Disclosure

  • We do not sell or rent your personal data.
  • We only share Bluetooth control commands with your paired hardware.
  • Anonymous analytics may be shared with our developers or service providers under strict confidentiality.

4. Security

  • All sensitive data (login credentials) are stored in Apple’s Keychain.
  • We follow Apple’s best practices for Bluetooth and local data storage.
  • We use HTTPS for any web-based requests.

5. Your Choices

  • Bluetooth permission: You can revoke at Settings → Bluetooth.
  • Analytics: You may disable usage sharing in the app’s Settings (if enabled).

6. Children’s Privacy

  • Our app is not directed to children under 13.
  • We do not knowingly collect information from children.

7. Changes to this Policy

We may update this policy to reflect app changes. We’ll post the new policy here and update the Last updated date.
